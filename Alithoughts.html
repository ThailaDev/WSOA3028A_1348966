Why skateboarding is currently not inclusive

Skateboarding was and to some extent still is male dominated. The skateboarding community currently only comprises of around 9 - 25% women depending on the source.

Skateboarding is an extreme sport, and many people still think that women are too fragile to participate in it. When it comes to skateboarding men and women’s biological differences do not matter, both have the right equipment to get on a board. It is just due to the socially constructed notion that women cannot take a beating that many still believe they do not have a place in skateboarding.

In the 70’s and 80’s there was a big fall in the number of skaters in general due to many of the parks closing and street skating being banned in most places. In the 90’s there was a resurgence but skateboarding was mostly marketed to men under the age of 18 which is very off putting to many women because going to a park and knowing you’re most likely to be the only woman there made the scene feel hostile.

‘Women’s sports’ such as netball or tennis reinforces the notions of how women ‘should’ behave due to the rigid bodily control that it forced upon its players. When women were involved in sports that allowed them to be freer with their bodies, they were heavily criticized for being un-ladylike.

Since sports are mainly controlled by men even today, there is a big stereotype of the ‘masculinisation’ of women who play more ‘dangerous’ sports which could lead them to lesbianism. – This stereotype is not only sexist but also homophobic.

How can we, as women make skateboarding more inclusive for women and the LGBTQIA+ communities? Well as a start, forming area specific girl skate group initiatives allows girls to start skateboarding in a safe environment. One such group is the Girls Skate Johannesburg group which provides girls with boards and have frequent meetups. The Girls Skate Group also allows anyone from the LGBTQIA+ community to join as well.

Corona and the Virtual Pride

Hey my dudes and dudettes and everyone in between or outside of! We are currently in the middle of a lockdown in South Africa, due to Covid19 and I am craving to go skating.

Anyways I just wanted to make a shoutout to the gay TikTok community for keeping me sane during this time. I have made so many wonderful open-minded friends on there during this difficult time and they have helped to make me feel less lonely.

As you all probably know by now most Pride events have been cancelled this year but some awesome individuals from TikTok, namely @fx.kiara.fx decided to host a virtual Pride during Pride Month.

Although we cannot celebrate face to face we can celebrate through the power of the internet via streams and podcasts and online challenged and I am so thankful for that!

Feelings

Hey my lovelies today is a bit dark and I wish I could find some light.

I have been in lockdown for almost two months now and I have started to see the old black dog. I miss normalcy and being able to skateboard. I have gotten so unfit during this time.

I have been lacking motivation to do anything and on top of all of that I have not been getting paid because obviously I cannot work at the Skatepark during lockdown.

I find a little bit of joy from the amazing friends I have made online, but I just wish I could see them face-to-face.

I miss the world like it once was, but I am still hoping that the world is different for the better after all of this.

Delta Park and Puppies

Hey guys! I just wanted to share my newfound appreciation for Delta Park in Johannesburg. It is one of the only parks in Johannesburg that are open during the pandemic and it has made lockdown so much easier for me. If it were not for Delta Park, I would just be stuck indoors all day long.

I take my dogs there several times a week now (while wearing a mask of course) and its just so nice to see people other than the ones I live with. Before lockdown I did not really go to Delta Park very often even though I live close to it.

I have met so many wonderful people there while walking around and my dogs get to make some friends with other dogs. It is very important to socialise your dogs while they are puppies and mine are less than six months old and we got them just before lockdown so Delta has made a huge difference in their socialisation!!

Johannesburg Pride Update

Hey everyone!! A few days ago, the Johannesburg Pride Facebook page added an announcement that Pride will be moving to an online platform with hopes that the actual march will still be able to happen in person.

They did mention that if they do not meet regulation standards that they will cancel the physical march, however throughout the month of October (which is South Africa’s official Pride month) they will be hosting live events with creators from all over South Africa.

I am excited for this news as I was scared that they would just cancel the event completely. Pride is so important to me as I am part of the LGBTQIA+ community and love to spend time with likeminded people at Pride events. At least this way we can still interact on a virtual platform.

As the global pride event ends soon, I am so excited for South Africa’s pride month. I Think it will bring some light to our beautiful country despite all the terrible stuff that is currently happening all over the world.

Also here is a reminder that Pride was started by Queer women of colour during the Stonewall Riots!! Do not forget.

Exercise during a Pandemic

Sup!

I have not been able to go skating since lockdown started and I have had very little motivation to get up off my butt and do some exercise, however I have had a friend essentially force me to do Yoga with her through an app and I am actually really grateful for that.

The Yoga has at least helped with my stiffness that I have acquired by spending most of my time behind my computer during these times.

I really do recommend downloading a training app even if its just a Yoga app and doing at least some physical exercise every day. It will make your body feel a lot better.

It is impossible to reach 10 000 steps at home but thankfully when I go walking at Delta Park I can get those steps in. So, if you have a park near you, go walk around for a couple of hours. Your body will heavily appreciate it!
